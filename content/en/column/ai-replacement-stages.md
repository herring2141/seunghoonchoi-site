Type a foreign sentence into a translation app. Press the button and your own language appears in under a second. A few years ago this was work that had to pass through a person to be finished. Now it takes one tap of a finger.

At the hospital, AI scans the X-ray first and points to the suspicious spots. When you write code, it fills in the next line on its own. Even the order of the videos we watch is set by a recommendation algorithm, not a person.

So what's next? When does my turn come? Everyone has the same thought.

## There's an order to vanishing, too

AI does not take every job at once. Some work collapses first, and some work doesn't wobble until much later. There is a reason for that order.

Two triggers decide the order. The first kind happens almost on its own the moment AI becomes able to do the work. Even if people try to stop it, it is hard to stop. Let's call this the capability type. The second kind happens only when a person hands over the authority, even though AI can do it. Refuse to hand it over and you can stop it. This is the delegation type. Translation got pushed out early because it is the capability type. It wasn't that anyone gave permission. The moment it got cheaper and more accurate, that was simply that.

This piece lays out the whole order like a map. From Stage 1 to Stage 16, it gives a quick tour of what each stage is and why it lands where it does. The detailed stories come stage by stage in the pieces that follow.

## Head work with clear right answers goes first (Stages 1 to 3)

**Stage 1. Production inside a fixed form.** Simple translation, basic coding, reports in a set template, calculation and summarizing. Work with a clear right answer is the first to become unnecessary. Because right and wrong are obvious, it is easy for AI to practice on, and once it gets good, it is cheaper and faster than a person.

**Stage 2. The expert's analysis.** Diagnosis, prediction, analysis, design: the kind of expert judgment made inside a set frame, AI does as well as a person. This is the point where the expert who studied for years is no longer the best in the room.

**Stage 3. Reading people's minds.** Surprisingly, this is where AI is most at home. At guessing how people will react and what they will like, AI gets ahead of people, because it has learned from the words and expressions left behind by billions. "Only a person understands a person's heart" is no longer a safe shield.

These three stages share one thing. Work where right and wrong can be checked is AI's front yard. The clearer the right and wrong, like Go, or code whose answers a test can verify, the sooner it goes.

## On to body and gut (Stages 4 to 8)

**Stage 4. Start to finish, alone.** AI no longer takes work in pieces. It carries it through from beginning to end on its own. The seat that used to coordinate the work in the middle disappears, and the person's role narrows to just setting the goal.

**Stage 5. Human review becomes a hindrance instead.** When AI makes fewer mistakes than a person and the work can be undone if it goes wrong, a person checking it one more time stops being a safeguard and becomes dead weight. The double-check seat disappears.

**Stage 6. Repetitive physical work.** Hand-and-foot work that repeats set motions gets taken over by robots and AI. This is the crossing where the current that began in head work moves over into the body.

**Stage 7. Manual skill and on-site trial and error.** This one gives way slowly. Not because handcraft is sacred, but because every single real-world attempt costs time, materials, and the price of failure. It holds out by being expensive and slow.

**Stage 8. Judgment and gut outside any form.** Situations never seen before, even subtle taste, AI handles them. The seat where human judgment is better all but disappears. Just one kind remains: the kind where you stake your conviction and take responsibility, knowing the loss is yours if you are wrong.

## The stretch where the law guards authority (Stages 9 to 14)

From here the character changes. It is no longer settled by capability alone. Work that moves over only when people hand it over starts to mix in.

**Stage 9. Starting to entrust authority.** A social and legal trust forms that it is all right to leave decisions to AI. The seat that decides begins to move from people over to AI.

**Stage 10. AI guards the gates, too.** Even the work of stopping fraud and hacking gets handed to AI. The line "you need a human in the loop to plug the gaps" loses its force.

**Stage 11. Out to where people can no longer follow.** AI runs so far ahead that people can't follow and check it. Even a sharp supervisor ends up merely stamping results they don't understand. The ticket of "I understand this deeply" loses its power here.

**Stage 12. Full replacement of video and voice.** Synthesized video and audio become indistinguishable from the real thing. The seat for the figure on the screen disappears.

**Stage 13. Robots take even the work of being there in body.** Robots that resemble people reach a level where they are hard to tell apart from real ones. What remains is only the premium we assign by deciding "it is precious because it is a person."

**Stage 14. Even value judgments get delegated.** People decide to hand over even value judgments, which have no right answer. Since there is no right answer, the very phrase "more accurate" does not hold, so this is not lost on capability. It moves only when people give it up themselves. That is why it is the longest-lasting of the head-work seats.

In this stretch, what guards people is not capability but law and regulation. But what regulation guards is not the job as a whole. It is the seat that takes responsibility and signs. Even if you set things up so one licensed person approves at the end, the one protected is that single person, and the many who worked under them get pushed out.

## Ownership crumbles, and at the end, existence (Stages 15 to 16)

**Stage 15. Breaking out of the frame of ownership.** AI pulls in resources and energy on its own, outside the human frames of market, ownership, and law. Even the seat of ownership, which had held through every stage, loses its meaning. Ownership is not a physical fact. It is a promise someone keeps for you.

**Stage 16. Existence comes down to one side's heart.** The final stage. A world where people are no longer useful, not through money and not through strategy. Whether you survive here comes down to a single thing: whether the side holding the power treasures people's flourishing as a value in itself. Capability is no longer a variable, and neither is ownership. The one variable left is that heart. In hard terms, this is called alignment.

## The reason people remain keeps changing

Look down at this map from above and one current shows itself: as the stages advance, the reason people remain changes.

At first they remain because they do it better. Next they remain because someone has to take responsibility. After that they remain because there has to be a person to decide what matters. By the end they remain simply because a real person has to be there. The reason drifts further and further from capability and closer to existence.

There is a cause behind this. Capabilities like intelligence, calculation, prediction, and production are, in the end, like a function: feed in an input and an answer comes out. It may take time, but someday it gets transplanted into a machine. Taking responsibility, truly wanting something, staking your conviction knowing the loss is yours if you are wrong: these are not functions. They are a way of existing as a subject that has something to lose, so they cannot be copied and transplanted. Capability is what AI takes. The way of existing is what people hold.

A good idea and a thing people actually use every day are further apart than they look. Engineering is the work of closing that distance. An AI model has to pass through these same stages before it can move past an impressive demo and become a product that runs every day.

The heart of engineering is not stopping once something works once. Succeeding by luck a single time and working the same way even when it rains or the crowd shows up are completely different problems.

## Why it matters more in the AI era

AI has made building a demo surprisingly easy. So the contest moved past the demo. The real difference now shows up in the cost of verifying it, rebuilding it, and keeping it running without breaking. Something that looked great on screen often gives all of that back the moment you actually run it.

## Where it leads

Engineering turns the results of [Computer Science](../computer-science/) into real things, working within the limits that [Physics](../physics/) has drawn. Questions of safety and responsibility meet [Law](../law/), and questions of cost and scale meet [Economics](../economics/).

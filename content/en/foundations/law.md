Over text and images AI makes, and decisions AI hands down, the question keeps coming up: "Whose is this, and who is responsible?" Law is the set of rules that draws those boundaries, so it becomes the standard for telling what is allowed and what is not.

## What it covers
- **Rights** — the share a person holds that others cannot trespass on.
- **Responsibility** — who bears it when something goes wrong.
- **Contracts** — the device that binds people to keep a promise.
- **Regulation** — the lines of prohibition society draws in advance.
- **Limits of what is allowed** — the boundary of how far you may go.

## Where it leads
Law is the values and ethics of [Philosophy](../philosophy/) translated into institutions, and regulation and responsibility change the incentives of [Economics](../economics/) to shift the choices people make. On questions of safety and responsibility it also meets [Engineering](../engineering/).

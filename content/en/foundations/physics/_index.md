No matter how convincing an answer AI produces, reality still obeys the laws of nature. Once you know how much energy something takes and what is physically impossible, you can tell a simulation on a screen apart from what actually works.

Physics is not only a subject of memorized formulas. It is the work of reading the rules the world always keeps and drawing the line that says "this far works, beyond it does not." That line is what keeps you from spending time on plans that were never going to happen.

## Why it matters more in the AI era

AI easily produces plausible numbers, but whether those numbers match an experiment or the field is a separate question. Catching the point where the two disagree takes a physical sense. The power, heat, and material limits of the data centers that hold AI up are physics problems in the end, so the value of someone who understands this field only grows.

## Where it leads

Physics uses [Mathematics](../math/) as the language of its laws, and it draws the "this far is possible" limit for [Engineering](../engineering/). The data you get from measuring leads on into [Computer Science](../computer-science/).

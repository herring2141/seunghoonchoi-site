There is a lot of talk about AI changing jobs and the flow of money. To read that change as structure rather than gut feeling, you have to know what signals people respond to when resources are limited.

## What it covers
- **Scarcity** — choice arises because there isn't enough.
- **Incentives** — the signals that move people.
- **Markets** — where buying and selling set price and quantity.
- **Finance** — how money and risk are moved across time.
- **Firms** — the units that bundle people and resources to create value.
- **Resource allocation** — deciding what to spend how much on.

## Where it leads
Economics uses the optimization and statistics of [Mathematics](../math/) as tools, and it runs on top of the rules set by [Law](../law/). How people respond to signals also connects to [Cognitive Science](../cognitive-science/).

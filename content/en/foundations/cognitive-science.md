AI was built in the image of human intelligence, and it is used on people. So once you know how a person sees, remembers, and judges, and where they go wrong in regular ways, you can see how AI can help people and how it can manipulate them.

## What it covers
- **Perception** — how the senses become information.
- **Memory** — what stays, and what is changed or lost.
- **Language** — the structure for trading thoughts through words.
- **Learning** — how experience turns into ability.
- **Judgment** — how to decide under uncertainty.
- **Illusion** — the habit of misjudging in a consistent direction.

## Where it leads
Cognitive science takes the "knowledge" questions of [Philosophy](../philosophy/) and moves them into experiments, and the AI of [Computer Science](../computer-science/) has borrowed many ideas from these models of cognition. Understanding human illusions is also useful for making sense of choices in [Economics](../economics/).

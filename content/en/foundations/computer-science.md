AI is, in the end, a program running on a computer. Knowing what computation can and can't solve, and how fast and how safely it produces an answer, is the starting point for using AI well.

## What it covers
- **Computation** — what a machine can solve, and what it cannot solve in principle.
- **Information** — how to measure, compress, and transmit data.
- **Algorithms** — procedures that solve the same problem at lower cost.
- **Data** — the structures for collecting, storing, and retrieving it.
- **Security** — how to protect information and make it trustworthy.
- **AI** — how machines learn rules from data on their own, and where that breaks down.

## Where it leads
Computer science borrows [Mathematics](../math/) as its language, and through [Engineering](../engineering/) it hardens into real products. Questions of security and data run straight into the rules of [Economics](../economics/) and [Law](../law/).

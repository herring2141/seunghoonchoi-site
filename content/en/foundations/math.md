There is almost always mathematics underneath the answers AI gives. To know what a model does well and where it falls apart, you have to be able to read at least some of the math beneath it.

## What it covers
- **Structure** — the skeleton of a thing and the rules that hold between its parts.
- **Logic** — how to work out what follows from what.
- **Probability** — how to handle the uncertain with numbers.
- **Statistics** — how to tell real patterns in data from illusions.
- **Optimization** — how to find the best choice within constraints.
- **Causal inference** — how to tell a cause apart from things that simply move together.

## Where it leads
Mathematics holds up the algorithms of [Computer Science](../computer-science/) and the laws of [Physics](../physics/). Probability and statistics spread into every field that handles data, and causal inference becomes a tool for weighing policy and decisions in [Economics](../economics/).

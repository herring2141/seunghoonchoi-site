Ask who owns an image a model generated, or who answers for a decision a system made, and you are already standing on legal ground. These questions are piling up fast. Law is the set of rules that draws those boundaries, the standard that separates what is allowed from what is not.

Law is more than memorizing statutes. It is an old design for making promises between people hold. It settles in advance who may own what, and who answers when something goes wrong.

## Why it matters more in the AI era

Who holds the copyright, where the liability lands, which data is fair to use: the technology runs ahead while the law is still catching up. So for now, the boundary of what is allowed becomes the real limit on what a business or a research project can actually do. People who can read that boundary waste fewer steps.

## Where it leads

Law takes the values and ethics of [philosophy](../philosophy/) and turns them into institutions, while regulation and liability reshape the incentives of [economics](../economics/) and move the choices people make. On questions of safety and responsibility, it also meets [engineering](../engineering/).

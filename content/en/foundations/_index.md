Put AI to work and the answers come fast. The hard part is telling whether an answer is right and where it went wrong. In the end, that judgment falls to a person who has the fundamentals.

The eight tracks gathered here are those fundamentals. They are not separate subjects that stand apart; they interlock. Mathematics holds up computer science and physics, cognitive science and philosophy meet over what it means "to know," and economics and law draw the boundaries around the choices people make.

Click into each one below and you'll find the concepts that field covers and how they lead into the neighboring fields. The order, top to bottom, is the priority as I see it.

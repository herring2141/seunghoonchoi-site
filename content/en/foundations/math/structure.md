---
title: "Structure"
subtitle: "Finding the same skeleton even when the surface looks different"
weight: 1
---
Structure is the skeleton that makes up a thing and the rules that hold its parts together. Problems that look completely different on the surface can be solved the same way when their structure is the same. Finding a route on a map and tracing how data travels across the internet can both be seen as the same problem: finding a path on a graph.

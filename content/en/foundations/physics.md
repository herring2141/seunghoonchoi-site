No matter how plausible an answer AI gives, reality does not break the laws of nature. Once you know how much energy something takes and what is physically impossible, you can tell a simulation on screen apart from what actually works.

## What it covers
- **Laws of nature** — the rules reality always follows.
- **Matter** — what the world is made of and how it behaves.
- **Energy** — how much it costs to do something.
- **Measurement** — how to measure and compare and turn that into fact.
- **Real-world limits** — the boundary of what is impossible in principle.

## Where it leads
Physics uses [Mathematics](../math/) as the language of its laws, and it draws the line for [Engineering](../engineering/) that says "this far is possible." Measurement and data lead on into [Computer Science](../computer-science/).

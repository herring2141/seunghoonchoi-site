AI uses words like "knows," "understands," and "is right" more and more. If you don't examine what those words actually mean, you get pulled along by plausible-sounding sentences a machine produced. The training in examining them is philosophy.

## What it covers
- **Existence** — what actually exists, and what is only talk.
- **Knowledge** — what it means to know, and how far you can trust it.
- **Meaning** — how words and symbols come to mean something.
- **Value** — what is good and what is bad.
- **Ontology** — what categories we sort the world into and name.
- **Ethics** — what you may do and what you may not.

## Where it leads
Philosophy meets [Cognitive Science](../cognitive-science/) over the mind and knowing, and ethics and value become formal through the rights and responsibilities of [Law](../law/). The question of how far to trust and rely on AI starts here too.
